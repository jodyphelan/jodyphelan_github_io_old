About

I am a research fellow at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine.

I am looking at the genomic diversity of Mycobacterium tuberculosis using next generation sequencing as part of the pathogenseq group under the supervision of Prof. Taane Clark

My research entails the design and implementation of data analysis pipelines to process and collate terabytes of data from various structured and unstructured sources to provide biological insights such as population structure, transmission and drug resistance

My CV can be downloaded here.

Projects

Mycobacterium aurum
The draft genome

Sequencing, assembling and annotating the draft genome of Mycobacterium aurum, a surrogate organism for high throughput screening of anti-mycobacterial agents.

PE/PPE genes
Characterising this diverse gene family

Assembling high coverage isolates to profile the variation in the highly repetitive PE and PPE genes.


Variability of sequencing
Sequencing for in-silico DST

We analysed the variability and reproducibility of two next generation sequencing platforms with an application to detect drug resistance.

Bacterial GWAS
Application to drug resistance

We describe a GWAS approach to detect variants associated with drug resistance in isolates from the TDR strain bank.

TBProfiler
A command line version

Currently in the process of implementing a command line version of the TBProfiler developed by Coll et al.
